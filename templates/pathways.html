Pathways

CS (or CS related) Degree

A university degree in computer science is the most strait forward way to get a job in software engineering. A degree focuses heavily on the theory behind computing, and many math courses would be required in a computer science program.

Pros

  • The production quality is much higher than that of a bootcamp.
  • Employers are more likely to hire someone with a degree than someone without.
  • You become skilled at programming in general (practical, theoretical, and mathematical); the skills you learn never go obsolete.
  • There are many more opportunities for internships than with the other routes.

Cons

  • It's the most expensive option by far.
  • It takes multiple years to complete.
  • There's a large amount of "fluff" in a degree.

Coding Bootcamp

A coding bootcamp is typically a 3 to 4-month program that teaches programming skills with the goal of landing the students a job. It normally teaches a particular set of technologies sought out by employers.

Pros

  • A bootcamp focuses less on the theory, and more on the practical skills you'll need on the job.
  • It is very quick to complete when compared to a degree.
  • Everyone taking the bootcamp with you is in the same position as you.

Cons

  • While cheeper than a degree, bootcamps are still very expensive: tens of thousands of dollars.
  • The skills learned in a bootcamp could go obsolete.
  • A certificate for completing a coding bootcamp holds little to no weight on a resume.

Self-Taught

Being a self-taught programmer is just an elimination of the other two routes. It means, there was no university or bootcamp involved in the learning process. Self-taught programmers can learn from online courses, youtube tutorials, and reading documentation.

Pros

  • This route is the cheapest by far; the only costs come from online courses.
  • It's completely self-paced; you have the freedom to decide what you want to learn and when you want to learn it.
  • The higher up your role is, the less not having a degree will matter; at higher roles, they only care about the actual skill you possess.

Cons

  • You have no guidance; you need to figure things out on your own and motivate yourself.
  • It would be harder to get a job interview.
  • There aren't any other students in the class to help you out; there is no class.
